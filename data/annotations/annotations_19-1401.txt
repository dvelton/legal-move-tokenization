PETITIONER BRIEF -- SECTION I
"RESPONDENTS FAIL TO REFUTE THE EXISTENCE OF A CIRCUIT SPLIT WARRANTING REVIEW"
================================================================================

ARGUMENT UNIT P-I-1
Text: "Petitioners demonstrated the existence of a circuit split. The Third,
Eighth, and Ninth Circuits have held that allegations that a defined-contribution
plan paid or charged to participants excessive fees (including recordkeeping and
investment management fees) suffice to support a claim for violation of ERISA's
duty of prudence."

  DOCTRINAL:    INVOKE_AUTHORITY
                Three circuits (3d, 8th, 9th) cited as establishing the rule
                petitioners favor. The move is not to analyze the holdings but to
                stack them -- three circuits agreeing creates implicit weight.

  NARRATIVE:    AFFIRMATIVE_FRAME
                Opens by declaring the split "demonstrated," treating the
                petitioner's position as already proven and placing the burden of
                disproof on respondents from the first sentence.

  FACT-FRAMING: FOREGROUND
                The specific fee types (recordkeeping and investment management)
                are foregrounded immediately to anchor the case in concrete,
                relatable economic harm.

  STYLISTIC:    COMPRESSION
                Dense, single-sentence summary of three circuits' holdings.
                Compresses complex appellate history into one assertion.

----------

ARGUMENT UNIT P-I-2
Text: "The Seventh Circuit rejected a claim based on such allegations, holding
instead that petitioners' claims were precluded by the potential to craft a
prudent explanation for respondents' conduct from petitioners' allegations."

  DOCTRINAL:    NARROW_HOLDING
                Petitioners characterize the Seventh Circuit's holding narrowly
                and unfavorably -- not as a reasonable reading of the pleading
                standard, but as an outlier requiring a "potential to craft a
                prudent explanation." This framing makes the holding sound like
                an invention rather than an application of existing law.

  NARRATIVE:    FRAME_AS_ERROR
                The word "rejected" and the phrasing "potential to craft a
                prudent explanation" are chosen to make the Seventh Circuit's
                approach sound intellectually dishonest -- the court was
                fabricating defenses for the respondents rather than evaluating
                the complaint.

  FACT-FRAMING: RECHARACTERIZE
                The Seventh Circuit's reasoning (that the complaint failed to
                state a plausible claim because alternative explanations existed)
                is recharacterized as something closer to burden-shifting -- the
                court "craft[ed]" explanations for the defendant.

  STYLISTIC:    CONCRETE_ANCHOR
                "Potential to craft a prudent explanation" is a specific,
                memorable phrase that petitioners use to pin a characterization
                on the Seventh Circuit's methodology.

----------

Text: "Respondents scarcely try to deny the existence of this circuit split.
The bulk of respondents' argument on the supposed lack of a circuit split is an
extended defense of the decision below on the merits, which, even if it were
correct (it is not, see infra Part II), would not refute the split's existence."

  DOCTRINAL:    DISTINGUISH_PURPOSE
                Petitioners separate two distinct questions: (1) does a circuit

                The parenthetical "(it is not, see infra Part II)" concedes
                awareness of respondents' merits argument but immediately
                redirects: even if that argument had force, it is irrelevant to
                the split question.

  FACT-FRAMING: FOREGROUND_ABSENCE
                Highlights what respondents did NOT do: they "scarcely try to
                deny" the split. The absence of a direct denial is treated as
                a concession.

  STYLISTIC:    UNDERSTATED_DEVASTATION
                "Scarcely try" is deliberately mild. The understatement implies
                that respondents' position is so weak they could not even mount
                a serious argument on this point.

  CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION: This unit directly responds to Respondent Section I,
  where respondents reframed the duty-of-prudence question rather than engaging
  the circuit-split question head-on. Petitioners call out this evasion
  explicitly.

PETITIONER BRIEF -- SECTION II
"THE SEVENTH CIRCUIT ERRED"
================================================================================

ARGUMENT UNIT P-II-1
Text: "The Seventh Circuit erred by dismissing the case when plaintiffs'
allegations gave rise to the plausible inference that the Plans' excessive fees
resulted from imprudent management."

  DOCTRINAL:    APPLY_STANDARD
                Petitioners invoke the Twombly/Iqbal plausibility standard.
                Their claim is procedural: at the motion-to-dismiss stage, all
                that matters is whether the allegations support a "plausible
                inference." They do not need to prove their case; they need only
                show the inference is plausible.

  NARRATIVE:    FRAME_AS_ERROR
                The section header and opening sentence both declare error
                directly. No hedging, no buildup.

  FACT-FRAMING: FOREGROUND
                "Excessive fees" and "imprudent management" are foregrounded
                as the two operative facts the reader should carry forward.

  STYLISTIC:    COMPRESSION
                One sentence carries the entire thesis of Section II. Everything
                that follows is elaboration.

----------

ARGUMENT UNIT P-II-2
Text: "Respondents' defenses of the decision below only underscore that the
decision hinged on inverting the applicable pleading standard by accepting
respondents' inferences and disregarding petitioners' reasonable inferences."

  DOCTRINAL:    BURDEN_SHIFT
                This is the core doctrinal move of the petitioner's case.
                They argue the Seventh Circuit flipped the Twombly/Iqbal
                standard: instead of drawing inferences in the plaintiff's
                favor (as required at the motion-to-dismiss stage), the court
                drew inferences in the defendant's favor. This is a burden-shift
                argument -- the lower court applied the wrong directional
                presumption.

  NARRATIVE:    ESCALATE_STAKES
                "Inverting the applicable pleading standard" elevates this from
                a case-specific error to a systemic procedural concern. If the
                Seventh Circuit can invert the standard here, it can do so in
                any ERISA case.

  FACT-FRAMING: RECHARACTERIZE
                Respondents' defense is recharacterized not as a legitimate
                argument but as evidence that "underscore[s]" the very error
                petitioners are alleging. The defense becomes proof of the
                problem.

  STYLISTIC:    --

  CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION: Directly counters Respondent Section II-A, where
  respondents cite Twombly/Iqbal and Fifth Third to argue petitioners' complaint
  was insufficiently specific. Petitioners flip this: they agree Twombly/Iqbal
  governs, but argue the Seventh Circuit applied it backward.

----------

ARGUMENT UNIT P-II-3
Text: "For example, respondents argue (at 14, 25) that the Seventh Circuit
properly held that respondents' choice of recordkeepers was justified because it
allowed respondents to offer a specific annuity fund. Respondents argue (at
13-14) that, because such an inference could be derived 'from [petitioners']
complaint,' it was proper for the court to draw that inference in respondents'
favor to dismiss the lawsuit."

  DOCTRINAL:    APPLY_TO_FACTS
                Takes the abstract doctrinal point (inverted pleading standard)

  NARRATIVE:    CONCEDE_AND_REDIRECT
                Petitioners do not deny that the annuity-fund inference can be
                derived from the complaint. They concede the inference exists
                but redirect: the question is not whether it exists but whether
                it should be drawn in respondents' favor at the pleading stage.

  FACT-FRAMING: FOREGROUND
                The annuity fund is surfaced as the specific factual detail
                around which the legal dispute crystallizes. This gives the
                abstract pleading-standard argument a tangible reference point.

  STYLISTIC:    CONCRETE_ANCHOR
                "A specific annuity fund" is a particular, graspable detail
                that makes the respondents' justification sound both narrow and
                ad hoc.

----------

ARGUMENT UNIT P-II-4
Text: "But petitioners alleged facts plausibly supporting the inference that the
Plans paid excessive recordkeeping fees because of their imprudent failure to
pursue strategies -- such as competitive bidding, consolidation to a single
recordkeeper, or negotiation of fee reductions -- that other university plans had

  DOCTRINAL:    APPLY_TO_FACTS

                The three strategies (competitive bidding, consolidation,

  STYLISTIC:    PARALLEL_STRUCTURE
                "Competitive bidding, consolidation to a single recordkeeper,
                or negotiation of fee reductions" -- tripartite list structured
                to accumulate weight.

  out of thin air." Petitioners respond not by defending the $35 figure but by
  shifting to the process failures -- a different factual basis that sidesteps
  respondents' attack on the numerical benchmark.

RESPONDENT BRIEF -- SECTION I
"PETITIONERS AND THE GOVERNMENT MISCHARACTERIZE THE NATURE OF THE DUTY OF
PRUDENCE UNDER ERISA FOR PARTICIPANT-DIRECTED PLANS"
================================================================================

ARGUMENT UNIT R-I-1
Text: "Petitioners and the government argue that, although the Plans'
participants are responsible for selecting their own investments, ERISA authorizes
a damages action for breach of the duty of prudence based on marginal costs
differences in isolated investment options, regardless of their place in the menu
of available options."

  DOCTRINAL:    NARROW_STATUTE
                Respondents narrow the scope of ERISA's duty of prudence by
                emphasizing that participants in defined-contribution plans
                select their own investments. This narrows the fiduciary
                obligation: if participants choose, the fiduciary's duty is
                limited to menu design, not individual option pricing.

  NARRATIVE:    AFFIRMATIVE_FRAME
                By pairing "petitioners and the government," respondents
                acknowledge they face two adversaries but frame both as
                advancing the same flawed argument. The pairing also subtly
                signals that the government is not a neutral party but an
                aligned advocate.

  FACT-FRAMING: RECHARACTERIZE
                Petitioners' claim (excessive fees from imprudent management) is
                recharacterized as about "marginal costs differences in isolated
                investment options." "Marginal" and "isolated" are doing heavy
                lifting -- they make petitioners' claim sound trivial and
                cherry-picked.

  STYLISTIC:    NEW_MOVE_[DIMINISHING_MODIFIER]
                The words "marginal" and "isolated" are not neutral descriptors.
                They are chosen to minimize the significance of the cost
                differences at issue. This is a stylistic move that works by
                word choice rather than structure -- inserting adjectives that
                shrink the opposing argument.

  CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION: Directly counters Petitioner Section II, where
  petitioners frame the issue as "excessive fees resulted from imprudent
  management." Respondents reframe it as "marginal costs differences" --
  replacing "excessive" with "marginal" and "imprudent management" with a
  neutral description of investment-menu design.

                participants choose investments) from traditional trust
                arrangements (where trustees choose). The duty of prudence

  NARRATIVE:    FRAME_AS_ERROR
                Petitioners' reliance on trust-law principles is framed as a
                category error -- applying doctrine developed for a "different
                situation."

RESPONDENT BRIEF -- SECTION II
"THE COURTS BELOW CORRECTLY HELD THAT PETITIONERS FAILED TO PLAUSIBLY ALLEGE
A BREACH OF ERISA'S DUTY OF PRUDENCE"
================================================================================

ARGUMENT UNIT R-II-1
Text: "Even if this Court adopts petitioners' and the government's paternalistic
conception of the duty of prudence, the courts below still correctly concluded
that petitioners' allegations fail to state a claim."

  DOCTRINAL:    CONSTITUTIONAL_AVOIDANCE (structural analogue)
                This is an "even if" argument -- a doctrinal hedging move.
                Respondents argue in the alternative: even accepting
                petitioners' legal framework, the result does not change. This
                does not map perfectly to CONSTITUTIONAL_AVOIDANCE but shares
                its structure: avoiding a doctrinal question by winning on
                independent grounds.

                NEW_MOVE_[ALTERNATIVE_HOLDING_ARGUMENT]
                An argument that the result holds regardless of which legal
                standard the Court adopts. Distinct from constitutional
                avoidance because it does not involve constitutional
                interpretation, but the structural logic is identical: win on
                ground B without needing to resolve question A.

  NARRATIVE:    CONCEDE_AND_REDIRECT
                "Even if" concedes the possibility that petitioners' legal
                standard is correct. The redirect is immediate: it does not
                matter, because petitioners lose anyway.

  FACT-FRAMING: RECHARACTERIZE
                Petitioners' and the government's reading of the duty of
                prudence is labeled "paternalistic." This is a loaded
                recharacterization that imports a politically inflected
                critique -- the government is being overprotective and treating
                plan participants as incapable of making their own choices.

  STYLISTIC:    NEW_MOVE_[DIMINISHING_MODIFIER]
                "Paternalistic" does double duty: it is both a fact-framing
                recharacterization and a stylistic choice. The word is selected
                to trigger skepticism in a Court that has generally been hostile
                to government overreach in the private-sector context.

  CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION: This is a direct structural counter to Petitioner
  Section II. Petitioners argued the Seventh Circuit applied the wrong standard.
  Respondents respond: even under your preferred standard, you still lose. This
  neutralizes the petitioners' strongest doctrinal move by making the choice of
  standard irrelevant.

RESPONDENT BRIEF -- SECTION II-A
"ERISA REQUIRES PLAUSIBLE ALLEGATIONS OF COMPARATIVELY IMPRUDENT BEHAVIOR"
================================================================================

ARGUMENT UNIT R-IIA-1
Text: "The motion to dismiss is an especially 'important mechanism for weeding

                them.

  NARRATIVE:    AFFIRMATIVE_FRAME
                Respondents frame the motion to dismiss not as a procedural
                technicality but as a substantive protection against meritless
                litigation. The word "weeding out" imports a normative judgment:
                some claims should be eliminated early.

  FACT-FRAMING: --

  STYLISTIC:    STRATEGIC_QUOTATION
                "Weeding out meritless claims" is quoted from Fifth Third and
                deployed for its rhetorical force. The agricultural metaphor
                implies petitioners' claims are weeds, not flowers.

  CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION: Directly counters Petitioner Section II's argument

ARGUMENT UNIT R-IIA-2

alleging 'sufficient factual matter' that 'permit[s] the court to infer more

  DOCTRINAL:    APPLY_STANDARD
                Standard Twombly/Iqbal two-part test, laid out methodically.

  NARRATIVE:    --

  FACT-FRAMING: --

  STYLISTIC:    --
                This is workmanlike doctrinal exposition. No stylistic moves;
                the persuasion comes from the standard itself.

----------

ARGUMENT UNIT R-IIA-3
Text: "'[A] complaint [that] pleads facts that are "merely consistent with" a
defendant's liability' will not do. Nor will factual allegations for which there
is an '"obvious alternative explanation"' that is 'lawful.'"

  DOCTRINAL:    APPLY_STANDARD
                Continues the Twombly/Iqbal framework, but now respondents

                consistent with" liability is insufficient, and "obvious
                alternative explanations" defeat plausibility.

  NARRATIVE:    PREEMPTIVE_REBUTTAL
                By emphasizing the "obvious alternative explanation" prong,
                respondents preempt petitioners' argument that any inference
                from the complaint should be drawn in petitioners' favor.
                Respondents are setting up the argument that their conduct has

  FACT-FRAMING: --

  STYLISTIC:    STRATEGIC_QUOTATION
                Heavy use of nested quotations from Iqbal and Twombly. The
                density of quotation marks signals that this is not respondents'
                characterization but the Court's own words being deployed
                against petitioners.

ARGUMENT UNIT R-IIA-4
Text: "First, the complaint 'must plausibly allege an alternative action that
the defendant could have taken' -- i.e., an alternative action that was actually
available to the fiduciary. Second, the complaint must 'plausibly allege[] that
a prudent fiduciary in the same position could not have concluded that the
alternative action would do mor[e harm than good]...'"

  DOCTRINAL:    APPLY_STANDARD / NARROW_HOLDING
                Respondents apply Fifth Third's two-part test for ERISA
                breach-of-duty claims. But notice the narrowing: they emphasize
                "actually available" (the alternative must have been real, not
                hypothetical) and the "could not have concluded" formulation,
                which sets a demanding standard for plaintiffs.

  NARRATIVE:    AFFIRMATIVE_FRAME
                The two-part framework is presented as settled law, not
                respondents' preferred reading. By formatting it as "First...

CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION SUMMARY
================================================================================

1. THE PLEADING-STANDARD BATTLE
   Petitioners (P-II-2): The Seventh Circuit "inverted" Twombly/Iqbal by drawing
   inferences in defendants' favor.
   Respondents (R-IIA-1 through R-IIA-4): The standard inherently demands
   specificity. Petitioners' complaint is simply deficient.
   Assessment: Both sides claim the same standard supports their position. The
   doctrinal dispute is not about which standard applies but about its
   directionality at the motion-to-dismiss stage.

2. THE RECHARACTERIZATION WAR
   Petitioners frame the case as: excessive fees + imprudent management.
   Respondents reframe it as: marginal cost differences + available alternatives.
   Key terms:
     Petitioners: "excessive," "imprudent," "failure to pursue strategies"
     Respondents: "marginal," "isolated," "paternalistic," "naked assertion"
   Both sides are fighting to define the vocabulary the Court will use. Whoever's
   terminology sticks will likely win.

3. THE PROCESS vs. OUTCOME SPLIT
   Petitioners emphasize process failures (no competitive bidding, no
   consolidation, no fee negotiation) -- P-II-4.
   Respondents emphasize outcome deficiencies (no specific vendor named, no
   factual basis for the $35 figure) -- R-IIB-2 through R-IIB-4.
   These arguments talk past each other: petitioners say the process was
   imprudent; respondents say the complaint does not plausibly allege a better
   outcome was available.

4. THE CIRCUIT-SPLIT vs. MERITS DEFLECTION
   Petitioners (P-I-3): Respondents are conflating the split question with the
   merits question.
   Respondents (R-I): Reframe the duty of prudence itself, which is a merits
   argument embedded in what should be a split discussion.
   Petitioners correctly identify this maneuver in P-I-3.

5. THE DEFERENCE FRAMING
   Respondents (R-I-5): Cite Breyer's "at a distance" language to argue for
   fiduciary deference.
   Petitioners (P-II-2): Argue this deference was applied at the wrong stage --
   it may govern substantive review but should not govern pleading sufficiency.
   The Breyer quotation is respondents' strongest single rhetorical move in the
   available text.

NEW MOVES IDENTIFIED
================================================================================

1. NEW_MOVE_[DIMINISHING_MODIFIER] (found in R-I-1, R-II-1)
   Definition: Inserting adjectives or adverbs that systematically minimize the
   significance of opposing facts or arguments. "Marginal costs differences,"
   "isolated investment options," "paternalistic conception." These are not
   neutral descriptors; they are calibrated to shrink the opposing claim. Differs
   from RECHARACTERIZE in that the facts themselves are not re-described -- they
   are modified with diminishing qualifiers that change their perceived scale.

2. NEW_MOVE_[AUTHORITY_SIGNALING] (found in R-I-5)
   Definition: Citing a case not just for its holding but for the identity of
   its author, where the author's stature on the current Court adds persuasive
   force beyond the legal reasoning. The parenthetical identification of the
   judge (here, Breyer as Chief Justice) is the move itself. Standard
   INVOKE_AUTHORITY focuses on the holding; AUTHORITY_SIGNALING focuses on who
   said it and their current relevance.

3. NEW_MOVE_[ALTERNATIVE_HOLDING_ARGUMENT] (found in R-II-1)
   Definition: An "even if" argument that the outcome should be the same
   regardless of which legal standard the Court adopts. Structurally similar to
   CONSTITUTIONAL_AVOIDANCE but operates outside the constitutional context.
   The move neutralizes the opponent's doctrinal argument by making the choice
   of doctrine irrelevant to the outcome.

4. NEW_MOVE_[OPPONENT_CONCESSION_CAPTURE] (found in R-IIB-4)
   Definition: Identifying and foregrounding a concession made in the opposing
   brief and deploying it as evidence against the opposing party. Differs from
   CONCEDE_AND_REDIRECT (which describes a party's own strategic concession).
   Here, the concession belongs to the opponent and is used offensively.

5. NEW_MOVE_[REGISTER_CONTRAST] (found in R-IIB-3)
   Definition: Deliberate shift from formal legal register to colloquial or
   dismissive language ("plucks out of thin air," "vaguely gesturing") to signal
   that an argument is not worthy of serious technical engagement. The contrast
   between the surrounding formal analysis and the informal characterization is
   itself the rhetorical device.
