SECTION 5: Merits (IV. The decision below is wrong.)
================================================================

ARGUMENT UNIT 5A: Only the Eleventh Circuit's rule accords with Skinner
"Only the Eleventh Circuit's rule, which runs the limitations period from the end of state-court litigation, accords with Skinner."

  DOCTRINAL: APPLY_STANDARD — Applies Skinner's framework: when a plaintiff challenges a statute "as construed" by state courts, the claim cannot accrue until the state courts have authoritatively construed it.
  DOCTRINAL: NARROW_HOLDING — Reads Skinner as requiring that accrual follow authoritative construction, not the first adverse ruling.
  NARRATIVE: AFFIRMATIVE_FRAME — States the merits position as a straightforward application of existing law, not a request for new doctrine.
  FACT-FRAMING: RECHARACTERIZE — Reframes the Fifth Circuit's rule as requiring plaintiffs "to sue before they can definitely know what the statute means."
  STYLISTIC: COMPRESSION — "Only the Eleventh Circuit's rule" signals that the answer is singular and obvious.

ARGUMENT UNIT 5B: The Fifth Circuit's rule creates impractical parallel litigation
"The Fifth Circuit's rule creates a 'ticking limitations clock' requiring parallel litigation that 'run[s] counter to core principles of federalism, comity, consistency, and judicial economy.'"

  DOCTRINAL: DISTINGUISH_PURPOSE — Argues the Fifth Circuit's rule defeats the very purposes SOL doctrine is supposed to serve: orderly litigation, comity, and judicial economy.
  NARRATIVE: INSTITUTIONAL_REBUKE — Implicitly criticizes the Fifth Circuit for adopting a rule that this Court's own precedents (McDonough) have flagged as problematic.
  FACT-FRAMING: FOREGROUND — Puts the practical absurdity front and center: concurrent state and federal litigation over the same DNA evidence.
  STYLISTIC: STRATEGIC_QUOTATION — Deploys McDonough's language to frame the Fifth Circuit's rule in terms this Court has already condemned.

ARGUMENT UNIT 5C: Accrual requires a "complete and present cause of action"
"As McDonough reaffirmed, accrual occurs once there is 'a complete and present cause of action.'... a § 1983 plaintiff like Mr. Reed knows the fact and extent of his harm only based on the authoritative construction of state law."

  DOCTRINAL: APPLY_STANDARD — Applies McDonough/Wallace's "complete and present cause of action" test to show Reed's claim was incomplete until the CCA ruled.
  DOCTRINAL: BURDEN_SHIFT — Shifts the analytical burden to Goertz: if the claim isn't complete until authoritative construction, Goertz must explain why accrual should predate that event.
  NARRATIVE: CONCEDE_AND_REDIRECT — Concedes Reed may have "suffered harm" from the original denial but redirects: that harm "isn't certain, ripe, or complete."
  FACT-FRAMING: SEQUENCE — Reconstructs the timeline to show each step building toward a complete cause of action, with the CCA's decision as the triggering event.
  STYLISTIC: STRATEGIC_QUOTATION — Weaves together McDonough, Wallace, and Skinner quotations to construct a wall of authority supporting the accrual-at-authoritative-construction rule.

  CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION: Directly rebuts Respondent's Sections 4-5. Respondent argues the cause of action was complete at the trial court stage; Petitioner insists Skinner's "authoritative construction" language requires appellate finality.

CROSS-BRIEF INTERACTION SUMMARY
================================================================

1. ACCRUAL DATE — THE CENTRAL CLASH
   Petitioner (Sec. 5A-C) vs. Respondent (Sec. 4-5):
   Petitioner argues accrual requires "authoritative construction" by the state's highest court (CCA), reading Skinner's framework as mandating appellate finality. Respondent reframes the question around "notice" of the constitutional injury and argues Reed had a complete cause of action at the trial court stage (2016) or, at latest, the CCA's initial opinion (2017) — either way, more than two years before filing. Petitioner deploys APPLY_STANDARD and STRATEGIC_QUOTATION from Skinner/McDonough; Respondent counters with DISTINGUISH (finality vs. notice) and OPPONENT_CONCESSION_CAPTURE (using Reed's own complaint).

2. CIRCUIT SPLIT — CONCESSION VS. MINIMIZATION
   Petitioner (Sec. 1) vs. Respondent (implied):
   Petitioner opens with OPPONENT_CONCESSION_CAPTURE and AGREEMENT_TRAP, pinning Goertz to his concession of the circuit split. Respondent's brief sidesteps the split's significance by arguing Reed loses under any circuit's rule given his timeline, effectively using CONCEDE_AND_REDIRECT at the strategic level.

3. JURISDICTIONAL BARS — SETTLED VS. LIVE
   Petitioner (Sec. 3) vs. Respondent (Sec. 1):
   Petitioner treats Rooker-Feldman, standing, and sovereign immunity as foreclosed by Skinner and rejected below, using FRAME_AS_ERROR and INVOKE_AUTHORITY. Respondent deploys ALTERNATIVE_HOLDING (three independent bars) and a NEW_MOVE_DILEMMA_CONSTRUCTION that forces Reed to choose which characterization of his injury to defend — each triggering a different fatal defect.

4. ABANDONED FACIAL CLAIM — NARRATIVE WARFARE
   Petitioner (throughout) vs. Respondent (Sec. 3):
   Respondent's Section 3 performs extensive STRATEGIC_QUOTATION from Reed's own merits brief to document the abandonment of the facial claim. This move has no direct Petitioner counterpart in the cert-reply brief, because Petitioner had not yet made the strategic shift at that stage. The move is forward-looking — designed to constrain Reed's arguments at the merits stage.

5. STAKES AND PURPOSE — COMPETING FRAMES
   Petitioner (Sec. 4) vs. Respondent (Sec. 2):
   Petitioner ESCALATES_STAKES with exoneration data and life-and-death consequences; Respondent counters by BACKGROUNDING those equities and FOREGROUNDING SOL policies (diligent prosecution, repose, faded memories). Each side selects the policy frame most favorable to its position.

6. SKINNER — CONTESTED MEANING
   Both sides claim Skinner supports their position. Petitioner reads Skinner as establishing that the claim "turns on whether state law is unconstitutional as construed by the state courts" — requiring appellate finality for accrual. Respondent reads Skinner as merely establishing that § 1983 is the proper vehicle for DNA-testing claims, without prescribing accrual rules. This is ANALOGIZE vs. DISTINGUISH applied to the same precedent.

NEW MOVES IDENTIFIED
================================================================

1. NEW_MOVE_DILEMMA_CONSTRUCTION
   Definition: Framing the opponent's position as a forced choice between two or more characterizations, each of which triggers a separate fatal defect. Unlike a simple reductio, this move does not show the opponent's position is absurd — it shows the opponent cannot escape defeat regardless of which version of their argument they advance. Structural form: "If X, then [fatal consequence A]. If instead Y, then [fatal consequence B]."
   Found in: Respondent Section 1, Unit R1B.

2. NEW_MOVE_STRATEGIC_SHIFT_EXPOSURE
   Definition: Documenting and calling attention to the opponent's mid-litigation change in legal theory, framing it as tactical maneuvering rather than legitimate refinement. Distinguished from FRAME_AS_ERROR (which attacks the substance of a position) because this move attacks the evolution of the position itself — the shift is the evidence of weakness.
   Found in: Respondent Section 3, Unit R3A.
